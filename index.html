Literature Survey

Communication is the process of passing information from one person to another. The majority of the time, individuals communicate with signs and speech. Ordinary people communicate/interact with one another using natural language, whereas deaf and dumb persons employ tactile sign language. In today's technology, Covid 19 circumstances, all industries are turning to internet platforms to maintain their advancement. Because of the fierce competition in every sector, those with impairments have a tough time standing in the race. We can always encounter this problem through internet platforms such as online conferencing. People use sign language movements to convey their thoughts and emotions, and non-signers find it incredibly difficult to grasp, thus skilled sign language interpreters are required in any conferencing circumstance, such as daily meetings, educational, and training sessions. A third-party individual at the meeting is such an interruption, and it is not a feasible option. As a result, there has been an increase in demand for translating services during the last five years.

To overcome this, we recommend using a video-conferencing tool that recognizes sign language motions. This study has three facets, and the combination of each of these features yields a precise result: Artificial Intelligence, Gesture Recognition, and Sign Language.

  • Artificial intelligence is described as a system's capacity to respond appropriately to collect and understand information in order to achieve particular goals. There are three sorts of intelligence: reasoning intelligence, mechanical intelligence, and emotional intelligence. We can distinguish the dynamic presentations of a sign language interpreter using feeling intelligence models. This removes the primary impediment to creating coherent statements using identified items in an English phrase.
  • While focusing on computer vision-based SLR (Sign Language Recognition) approaches, the only device that can record the motions is a main camera. Computer vision-based approaches only require a lot of computing power. RNN models fed with a human optical-flow representation for movies based on posture estimation may be used to create a real-time sign language recognition system using a CNN.
  • Machine learning is a computer science method based on mathematics. It can learn algorithms without having to program" Arthur Samuel, 1959". Artificial Intelligence is used here in the form of "hidden pattern data." Machine learning predicts proper output based on past data. A neural network functions similarly to a human brain. This neural network is used to represent language processing in ways that are more complex than N-gram models.
  • An integrated video-conferencing voice transcription and speakers identification system prototype was designed for the globally recognized financial company to transcribe audio recordings to text while identifying the speakers of the meeting, and it is capable of producing a text-based output for later use. This system maintains a database to store the produced text-based output as well as the audio record. Since the organization relies on the WebEx Microsoft plug-in for video conferencing and meetings, the solution also should be compatible to work with WebEx.

Research Gap

Online communication is done using different approaches. However, video conferencing is more popular than ever because of the covid-19 pandemic situation. Since the pandemic situation, people cannot meet physically; if someone is infected with the virus, they have to be quarantined in their home. Thus, video conferencing technologies and related technologies are being developed rapidly in an unprecedented manner since it is widely used to overcome communication issues.

The novelty of research is to see how effective it will be in building an artificial intelligence and computer vision-based computer software to recognize sign languages, particularly American sign language, and translate them into text form and regular english voice terms interpret as sign notations. It will be built an American sign language translation system based on human hand signs. As a result, deaf people throughout the world will be able to converse with hearing people who aren't familiar with sign languages. It is believed to be a viable solution to the linguistic barrier that exists between the deaf population and the general public.

We can identify the research gap component-wise and shows the research gap in-simple below.

  • When we go through the sign language representing techniques, we can identify some major key points of presenting, Face, Hands, body parts like shoulders and signing space. The face is mainly affected to represent the emotional state in sign language, a sentence like 'help me'. Hands are the primary object of representation. Signing space is critical parameter when we use computer vison. Because web camera is capturing the small area of window. Most of the previous research focuses on static sign notation recognition because real-time sign notation is more complicated than static sign notation detection.
  • Predict the meaning of English words. To convert text to voice, image processing and APIs are used. Some are currently working on it, while one was a near effort that was never finished, and anticipated sentences are not based on sign language translated words. Furthermore, the projected phrases are neither meaningful nor grammatically accurate. Only words by words are translated from text to voice. As a result of the research gap, an application that predicts meaningful and grammatically correct English sentences from text words and converts them to audio can be developed.
  • Sign language grammar, word order, and sentence structure are all up for debate. It doesn't have any predetermined guidelines on how to write. Each sign language differs from the others in many aspects, including syntax, semantics, grammar, morphology, and phonology, with syntax referring to the rules that determine the arrangement of gestures to build a word. It is up to semantics to decide whether or not a set of sentences has any grammatical significance. Grammar determines the structure of the language. Similarly, the morphology takes into consideration the different methods of generating a sentence.
  • Surprisingly, non-manual factors have a significant impact on a gesture's understandability. In ASL, for example, raised eyebrows express an open-ended inquiry, but furrowed eyebrows convey a yes/no query. Mouth movement may also represent the various sizes of a cup, as shown by executing the "cup" gesture with shifting mouth positions. Apart from that, the placement of a subject/object inside the sign space is critical, since the same subject/object may be referred to another sign notation with a finger pointing to the same area or with a facial expression

Research Problem

Many people have hearing impairments, and these people live around the globe. They need to communicate with normal people daily to get along with the current world. Since people with hearing impairments cannot hear the audio output, have language difficulties, and cannot communicate verbally. People communicate through verbal languages. However, the majority of the people cannot understand sign language. Most ordinary people are unfamiliar with expressing their expressions through sign language. People who communicate through only sign language will not be able to understand speeches delivered in verbal languages during video or audio conferencing. Due to the above reasons, current video conferencing tools are not as effective in solving communication issues.

Being able to develop a sign language translation machine is not a simple task. However, there are two common methods used to address this problem, namely;

  • Sensor-Based Sign Language Recognition and
  • Vision-Based Sign Language Recognition

In this proposed solution, developing a video conference tool is demonstrated falls under the computer Vision-Based approach, and sign language to text/speech conversion is done without any sensors. The proposed solution captures the hand gestures using the video stream utterly free of any cost.

When considering a video-conferencing tool compatible with American Sign Language, working with a live translation is challenging because the real-time image processing and machine learning processing modules and NLP modules are working behind the scenes. It is pretty challenging to do real-time inference using limited hardware resources, which can be denoted as another research problem.

Research Objectives

Our main objective is to overcome the communication barriers between a normal person and a deaf or hard-hearing person when they interact through a video conferencing tool.

Creating a brand-new video conferencing tool (As a media conference module, we use Agora. We will not implement video stream servers along with this.), which translates American sign language notations to English and vice versa. First, we identify sign language notations spoken by a user using web cameras and image processing mechanisms as input.

Then, it is subjected to translation and accurate processes using various machine learning algorithms and interprets the output as audio and subtitles. In the next part, we input English voice (audio format) and process it to translate to sign language notations accurately and display them using an Augmented reality Bot.

Methodology

There are communication issues when communicating with people who are physically disabled such as people who have a hearing impairment or are physically mute. Researchers and software developers have tried to develop an online communication tool as a solution to overcome this problem in past years. However, no proper solution has been introduced, and most researchers are still focusing on this matter. They still are finding a proper solution with the help of the latest technological approaches. However, the issue continues to receive much attention due to its accessibility, feasibility, and availability issues. Our solution tends to solve the problem by considering the two concerns. As shown in following figures, the solution tries to solve the problem by addressing two functionalities. The application consists of 2 main features.

  • Translates American Sign Language to English text and audio.
  • Translates English text or audio to American Sign Language.
